Conclusion:


The linear regression model developed to predict 'Sales' based on 'Temperature' demonstrates a strong fit to the dataset.


    

  • High R-squared Score (0.9897): This indicates that approximately 99% of the variance in 'Sales' can be explained by 'Temperature'. This is a very high value, suggesting that 'Temperature' is an excellent predictor of 'Sales' in this dataset.
    • 

  • Low Mean Squared Error (2.96): A low MSE indicates that the model's predictions are very close to the actual 'Sales' values, implying high accuracy.
    • 

  • Visual Confirmation: The scatter plot with the regression line clearly shows a strong positive linear relationship between 'Temperature' and 'Sales'. The red regression line closely follows the trend of the data points, further confirming the model's good fit.
    • 

  • Prediction Capability: The model is effective in predicting sales for new temperature inputs, as shown by the prediction for 21.5 degrees Celsius.
    • 



In summary, the linear regression model successfully captures the relationship between temperature and sales, providing accurate predictions and a clear understanding of how changes in temperature influence sales in this context.
